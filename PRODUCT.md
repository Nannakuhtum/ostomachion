# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual browser players on desktop and mobile, arriving from games.aakkagam.com. They play in short contemplative sessions (a commute, a coffee break, a late evening). No accounts, no installs. Some come for the history (Archimedes' Stomachion), most for a genuinely hard spatial puzzle. The job to be done: manipulate 14 pieces with precision and satisfaction until the square is tiled.

## Product Purpose

A single-player implementation of the Ostomachion, the ancient Greek 14-piece dissection puzzle attributed to Archimedes. One game on one screen: tile the 12×12 square. A Collection (the gallery of distinct solutions found, counted up to board symmetry toward the known 536) opens from a quiet counter sitting above the board; there are no tabs and no other modes. Success: a player loses an hour without noticing, wins once, and comes back to grow their collection.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, tactile, timeless. A museum artifact you are allowed to touch. Quiet confidence: the interface never shouts, never gamifies, never nags. The drama lives in the pieces and the moment of solving.

## Anti-references

- Casual-game gloss: glossy candy buttons, coin counters, star ratings, interstitial popups, "Great job!" toasts.
- Greek kitsch: cartoon columns, laurel clip-art, meme-Sparta typography.
- Anything that makes a 2,200-year-old puzzle feel like a free-to-play title.

## Design Principles

1. **Artifact, not app.** The board and pieces read as a physical object; UI chrome recedes to the edges and stays quiet.
2. **Precision under the fingers.** Placement is exact integer geometry; feedback is immediate and honest. Nothing wobbles unless the player is moving it.
3. **Respect the player's intelligence.** Purist by default: no hints, badges, streaks, or tutorials-as-walls. Difficulty options exist (starter placements) but never advertise themselves.
4. **Delight at the moment of mastery.** Celebration is reserved for wins and new-solution discoveries; everywhere else the interface is calm.
5. **Timeless over trendy.** Choices should look right in ten years. When in doubt, pick the option a museum catalogue would pick.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast throughout.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: springs, drag inertia, and win bursts collapse to instant/fade equivalents.
- Piece manipulation controls (rotate knob, flip button) are visible affordances, not hidden gestures.
